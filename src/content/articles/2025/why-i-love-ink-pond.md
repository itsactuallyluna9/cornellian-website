The Cornell College campus boasts many spots where the community can see wildlife, beautiful trees, and flowers. The campus staff do a good job of nurturing the plants around campus and maintaining the grounds. Often, the staff can be seen tending to the grounds and particularly the plants that help bring campus to life in the warmer months. There are flowers and plants all over the campus, and it is always wonderful to see these flowers in bloom, especially near the dorms. Having plants near the student body and where they live is a great way to connect students with nature and the campus they live on. Ink Pond provides a unique opportunity for nature because it is the only body of water on campus. While the campus itself hosts various birds like bluejays, robins, and northern flickers, the pond also draws in geese and a heron that can be seen near or in the water. Beyond birds, the pond also has turtles that can be seen resting on the floating platform in the middle of the pond, and even goldfish can be found swimming in a small part of the pond.

![Balancing Act {caption=Balancing Act} {credit=James Belding}](../../../images/articles/2025/ink-pond-balancing-act.jpg)

The heron is a large bird and quite striking to look at. It does not stick around when humans get close, but it can be very exciting to watch it from afar as it walks through the water of Ink Pond. While geese can be fairly common, maintaining a respectable distance can be a rewarding experience. A camera can get a very clear and detailed shot of these geese as they swim through Ink Pond’s waters or stand on the floating platform that rests in the pond. This opportunity can provide beautiful pictures to enjoy and share with friends and family. These geese can also be fun to watch. From swimming in the pond, diving into the water, plucking or cleaning their feathers, and even standing on one leg, these birds are very cool to see in person at Ink Pond.

![Northern Flicker {caption=Northern Flicker} {credit=James Belding}](../../../images/articles/2025/ink-pond-northern-flicker.jpg)

There are two benches and a picnic table near the pond that offer a good view of the pond and a comfortable place to sit. Nearby, are many trees where various kinds of birds that live on or near campus can be found flying around. The pond can also offer a good view of the top of King Chapel where a red-tailed hawk can sometimes be found. The view of King Chapel is valuable to have a chance to see red-tailed hawks, but owls have also been seen at the chapel and the pond. Birds like the northern flicker or the bluejay provide a colorful contrast to their surroundings. The northern flicker has bright yellow feathers on the underside of their wings and the blue feathers of the bluejay are eye-catching as they fly around. The sound of these birds on campus can be relaxing while reading, studying, or eating, and Ink Pond can often be a calm place to do that.

![Cedar Waxwing {caption=Cedar Waxwing} {credit=James Belding}](../../../images/articles/2025/ink-pond-cedar-waxwing.jpg)

Birds, turtles, and fish are not the only wildlife that can be found near Ink Pond. Like most of campus, squirrels can frequently be found at the pond. Squirrels seem to be something of a campus icon and the pond offers a good place to see campus’s fuzzy friends while relaxing on one of the benches.

The life of a college student—especially the life of a Cornell student—can be very busy. From classes and homework, to the many clubs and extracurricular activities that Cornell offers, there is always something to do. But sometimes it is nice to stop and appreciate the sights of campus.

Students have to travel the campus each day for classes, meals, and activities. By needing to travel across campus so often, students can forget to appreciate their surroundings. If a student is able to take the time to slow down and watch the nature of campus, there can be some wonderful sights. Ink Pond rests out past the Russell D. Cole Library, and can be out of the way of most students’ paths to and from their usual destinations. But, by taking time to stop by and be aware of the nature around them, students can appreciate the beauty of the leaves as they change color, the birds and their singing, and the uniquely beautiful sights that students will inevitably run across on their journeys around campus.
