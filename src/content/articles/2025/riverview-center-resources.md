Cornell’s Ebersole Health and Well-being Center does a great job of providing mental and physical health services to students at no (additional) cost. Ebersole has two counselors and a nurse available to students throughout the week. However, there is another resource available to the student body, yet many of us remain unaware that we have access to it.

The Riverview Center, located in Cedar Rapids, specializes in sexual assault support for those who need it. Advocates from the center are available to discuss options after an incident, accompany individuals to the hospital, or provide any further support that victims of these situations may need. Additionally, because advocates from the center serve as a confidential resource (meaning they are not mandatory reporters), Riverview provides support regardless of whether the assault has been reported or not, letting the individual decide how they are comfortable moving forward in their situation. As Cornell’s Ebersole staff does not currently include a sexual assault advocate, they are partnered with programs like the Riverview Center to expand the help students have access to.

Bailey Heathman, from the Riverview Center, comes to campus on a regular basis in order to bring these services to the students of Cornell. For students who wish to receive these services, she is on campus the second and fourth Wednesdays of every block from 11 a.m. to 1 p.m. in the Ebersole Health and Well-being Center. Students are welcome to email her at bailey@riverviewcenter.org or schedule an appointment with her through Ebersole on the days she is on campus; all one has to do is ask for the Riverview advocate. Of course, like any service at Ebersole, when Heathman is on campus, walk-ins are always welcome if there is availability.

The Ebersole Health and Well-being Center strives to look out for students in any situation they may face. By bringing other resources, like that of our on-campus Riverview advocate, they are furthering the support that Cornell is able to provide. Feel free to contact the staff at Ebersole or Riverview Center if you have any questions or are seeking help. Please use the resources when you need to, that’s what they’re there for!
