title: "Staff Highlight: Nicole Casal"
author: Ren Solis-Roman
pubDate: Nov 19, 2025
---
Nicole Casal is the Student Activities Coordinator for Cornell. She is a Cornell Alumna, class of 2018. Casal began working as the Assistant Director of Residence Life in August of 2021. But as of November 2024, she has been working to bring student events to life on campus.

Casal has always enjoyed planning events. As a student, she helped plan many different events for varying student organizations. A few examples would include PAAC, KRNL, and music programming for Cornell’s Theatre Department.

Emma Shipley ‘26, current president of PAAC, reflects, “Her enthusiasm and desire to see students succeed in different organizations has been one of her most admirable qualities. She is a dedicated person with valuable wisdom, and I couldn’t be more grateful for all the help she has given us in PAAC this past year.” Casal notices that post-COVID, students are more vocal and active on campus. She has seen the increase of spaces that allow students to interact with student activities more often than before. This is mainly because of the shift in the kinds of activities being offered. More student driven activities are being held around campus and as a result, there is more participation from students.

Along with Casal’s role as Student Activities Coordinator, she was a Posse Scholar mentor for Posse 1 (class of 2024) and Posse 3 (class of 2027). Casal looks back at her time with Posse fondly saying, “It was a breath of fresh air to the normal workload I had to manage.” She enjoyed being part of a community, rather than strictly leading one. Kevin Zazueta ‘27 says, “Nicole always showed that she cared about my well being. She’s the reason college has been so streamlined for me.” 

Casal’s outgoing message for Cornell students is, “Cornell is a place to make your own experiences. There are so many opportunities to find out what works best for you, and to create your own journey. I want to encourage students to, in the best way, do things for yourselves, in a way where you are filling your own cup. [You] should take this time to explore rather than following ideals or impressions previously made.”
