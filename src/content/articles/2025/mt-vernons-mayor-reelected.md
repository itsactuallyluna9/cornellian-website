---
title: Mt. Vernon's Mayor Reelected
author: Murray Page
pubDate: Nov 19, 2025
---
There were 1,219 ballots cast for the November 4, 2025 Mount Vernon mayoral election. This is about a 34% turnout rate for a city election, much higher than average. Current and recently reelected mayor, Tom Wieseler, says this is due to a huge unexpected gift of time that he received from a club on Cornell’s campus.

There were so many sameday voter registration forms that poll-workers had to pick up more physical forms twice on election day. The first time, they just drove to the other voting location in Mt. Vernon, St. John’s Catholic Church, but the second time, workers in Cedar Rapids delivered forms and some students waited at the polls for an hour for them to return. “I’m not sure I would have waited that long,” Wieseler says.

It is very rare for local elections to have more turnout than national elections and Wieseler argues that no other college had this much engagement in this kind of election. “You could write a case study about that,” he says.

Wieseler is passionate about college students’ right to vote in the election where their college is located. He argues that Cornell students pay more to the college than citizens pay for taxes. “Every vote counts and every vote is equal,” Wieseler says. He recognizes that the city makes decisions that greatly affect the college, including having the appropriate city resources to be prepared to assist the college in times of disaster. He is also aware of and thankful for students’ input in city decisions, jobs, and leadership positions before and after graduation.

Keith Huebner was the opponent to Wieseler in the mayoral election. Huebner ran on the campaign slogan “Reunite Mt. Vernon.” He was a MAGA supporter who had spoken about wanting to fly a confederate flag downtown instead of the flags for women’s history month, black history month, LGBTQIA+, etc. that are normally flown there. Wieseler argues, “my opponent was overboard in how he wanted to relate to immigration and ICE policies. He wasn’t even on the border, he was very strong about his opinions, dramatically over strong.” Wieseler received 83.8% of the vote. He says that was the highest winning percentage of any election he saw in eastern Iowa and that he united 83.8% of Mt. Vernon residents.

At one point Huebner had campaign flyers that contained Cornell College, Mt. Vernon school district, and City of Mt. Vernon logos. Wieseler said that right when these flyers hit the ground, the institutions were informed of the unauthorized use of their logos. “There are no secrets in Mt. Vernon,” Wieseler says.

Wieseler wants Mt. Vernon to be a place where students can feel safe and comfortable. “We should be accepting of all people,” he says. He says the city will continue to stand by the Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion statement they have made in the past. “We have a unique culture here, which I did not develop, but I am—by my position—a custodian of this culture; and, I need to respect what it is and do what I can to continue that culture of acceptance that is ingrained in our community,” Wieseler says.

There were also elections for Mt. Vernon city council and school board. Maggie Hartzler won the city council seat and will soon have a sit-down with Wieseler to discuss leadership, continuity, and LGBTQIA+ representation in the city council. They will be on the lookout for a bench of candidates for city council, specifically women. “I’m very aware that I want to maintain gender balance even if it doesn’t make the governor happy,” says Wieseler.

Wieseler was very thankful to the Students for Democratic Society (SDS) group on campus and the co-president of the group, Nick Alderman, for their help in mobilizing and informing students. Wieseler was also thankful to the administration for respecting students rights while not inappropriately engaging and letting students participate as they wished. He, of course, is also thankful to the students for showing up.

> Thank you to Cornell students and staff for re-electing me as mayor of Mt. Vernon. Your efforts were very much appreciated. All votes matter. - Mayor Tom Wieseler
