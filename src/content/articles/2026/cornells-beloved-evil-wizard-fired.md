title: Cornell's Beloved Evil Wizard Fired
author: Millarca Cornicus
pubDate: Apr 1, 2026
---

On Friday November 21st, Cornell College announced that it would no longer be offering major courses of study in many humanities departments, and with that, many of the staff in those departments would no longer be working at the college. However, recently uncovered information shows that Cornell’s administration is also firing the campus’ much beloved evil wizard, Erasmus the Cruel and Terrible. Student uproar at Erasmus’ firing cannot be understated with administration bringing back Saturday dinner in an attempt to appease the ever-growing mob of angry students. “Their outrage is understandable,” one Cornell staff member said, while barricading themselves in their office to avoid the mob with torches and pitchforks,

> Erasmus’ effect on campus cannot be understated, everything he’s done has been well-received by the students, he allowed the football team to lose 62-6, he’s been teaching the squirrels military tactics, and has personally ensured that all dorm buildings consistently smell like weed and body odor. But the students need to realize that they’re going to be living in the real world soon, they need to learn how to have their sports teams lose without outside influence and how to make their houses smell foul on their own. The absence of dark magic can be a blessing on campus if anything, the amount of both ghouls and dragons will decrease allowing the squirrels to act upon their villainous machinations unhindered, and besides all those adventurers seeking to cleanse the lands of darkness and magical evil will be bothering other colleges, allowing students to focus on their studies instead of aiding the forces of good.

While college administration assures us that campus can thrive without an evil wizard, many students have their doubts and continue to organize events to ensure that Erasmus’ time here at Cornell is not forgotten.
