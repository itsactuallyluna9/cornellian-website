After decades of research regarding the importance of critical thinking and one’s ability to craft a strongly-worded essay, the results are finally in: writing is dead. Given the mass integration of artificial intelligence into our daily lives, writing anything is now both utterly pointless and harmful. Emerging research funded by Elon Musk and Mark Zuckerberg is all pointing to the same conclusion: writing is detrimental to human efficiency because of the mental toll it takes on the brain. Thinking uses human energy that could otherwise be spent indulging in YikYak drama or shopping on Amazon and Shein.

But even though writing is now completely invaluable and an utter waste of time, your professors may not see it that way. When assigning work that requires immense effort, they may encourage you to take advantage of the Dungy Writing Studio, a free resource in which students can meet with one of 16 trained peer consultants to get assistance with anything from idea-generation and outlining to writing and revision. But why put the “brain” in “brainstorming” when artificial intelligence can do all of this for you? Using AI as an educational tool not only decreases human brain functioning and allows you to be more susceptible to persuasive information—it also comes at no personal cost! Sure, the large-scale use of AI is rapidly depleting the planet of its water, but we don’t need water anymore. As determined by top experts, sustaining human bodily functions like thinking and surviving will no longer be necessary since artificial intelligence can complete labor without us. Thus, human water intake can be reallocated to instead sustain the functions of AI data centers. You just keep guzzling those energy drinks to make it through the day; AI has got your back.

The Writing Studio is additionally purposeless because it serves as a calm, welcoming environment equipped with whiteboards, fidget toys, and Jolly Ranchers—all of which are completely unnecessary when AI can be used in the comfort of your very own, mold-infested, non-air-conditioned dorm room! With AI, you never have to venture out into the world to experience the horror of human-human interaction and erratic Iowa weather. You can have all of your writing and educational needs met at the touch of a button! And if you do experience loneliness (as so many people given the current state of the world), chatbots are friends in which you can always confide. Unlike your ex-best friend and that University of Iowa student you matched with on Tinder, AI will never leave you.

Ultimately, artificial intelligence can support both your educational and emotional needs, rendering all other pre-existing resources irrelevant. If you are to take anything away from this article, let it be this: the Writing Studio sucks, humans don’t need water, and tech billionaires can do no wrong.
