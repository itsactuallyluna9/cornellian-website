In November, 2025, Natalia’s Food Pantry was opened as a satellite to the SouthEast Linn Community Center (SELCC). SELCC’s main food pantry is located in Lisbon to serve the Lisbon-Mount Vernon area. Approximately 1 in 8 of Iowans face food insecurity regularly despite Iowa producing the highest agricultural output in the United States—second to California. Organizations like SELCC work within the local communities to provide food and necessities to anyone who walks in. As Natalia’s Food Pantry is a satellite location, SELCC stocks the pantry, provides staff training, and tracks incoming and outgoing food and donations for the pantry.

The director of Ebersole Health and Wellbeing Center, Marcia Sisk, explained that “the name Natalia honors an AmeriCorps member who was passionate about addressing food insecurity during her service with Cornell College.” Natalia conducted research and shared her ideas about opening a food pantry at Cornell. Unfortunately, her desire to open a food pantry on campus was not materialized before the end of her AmeriCorps service. The food pantry was named after Natalia to honor her vision and passion for addressing the food insecurity at Cornell.

With the removal of several weekend meals from Bon Appétit, Natalia’s Food Pantry is a great resource. The pantry is open for the Cornell community to access from the lower floor of Ebersole. Students, faculty, staff, and Cornell contracted employees can utilize any of the resources provided. There is a wide selection of canned and boxed food that can be selected from. Additional ly, hygiene products are available for anyone who would like them. A new clothing rack has been added recently with an expanding selection of clothes. Natalia’s food pantry is open to feedback and requests for specific items, although they can’t guarantee that they can provide that specific item. Requests can be made to Ebersole staff (Hannah Ganzel or Marcia Sisk) or to the student volunteers who staff the pantry.

Anyone who would like to get resources from the food pantry can enter through the living room, the main entrance of Ebersole, or through the entrance near Campus Safety in the back. Posted signs will direct you to the food pantry at the bottom of the stairs where a volunteer can help you with whatever you would like. Currently, the pantry is open on Mondays and Thursdays from 11 a.m. to 12:30 p.m. and from 5:30 to 7 p.m. These hours include the Thursday over block break. The food pantry will have slightly different hours over fall and spring breaks. However, if someone requests access outside of the scheduled hours, the food pantry can be opened for them. When visiting the food pantry, you will be asked for your Cornell ID number as a way to track how many students utilize the food pantry as well as weighing how much food is being used.

The food pantry is fully staffed by student volunteers and operates because of fundraising, food drives, and donations. Food and clothing drives are being held regularly around campus. SELCCalso holds drives and takes donations specifically for Natalia’s Pantry. Both Ebersole and SELCC are always open to donations that can be dropped off at either location. When donating to SELCC, let the staff know that the items are for Natalia’s Food Pantry at Cornell College.

Natalia’s Food Pantry was founded as a result of the hard work of Nicole Casal, Anna Butz, Hannah Ganzel, Miriam McConnell, Karla Steffens, Marcia Sisk and Nicole Alexander from SELCC. Additionally, the Mount Vernon Community largely provides donations to SELCC and Cornell’s food pantry.

On February 25th at 12:30 p.m., there will be a ribbon cutting ceremony to celebrate Natalia’s Food Pantry. If you would like more information about the pantry or how to volunteer or donate, please reach out to Marcia Sisk or Hannah Ganzel.
