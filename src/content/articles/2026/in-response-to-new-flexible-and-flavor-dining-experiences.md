After receiving numerous complaints about getting rid of Saturday dinners, Bon Appétit has decided to remove all meals. Breakfast, lunch, and dinner will no longer be offered in accordance to “new structural decisions” (Bone Appleteeth Cornell College). No refunds will be offered to students during the remaining semester. Instead, the money will go to feeding the creature that lives in the Thomas Commons air ducts. A representative from the cafeteria told us in an anonymous interview for fear of their safety, “he eats the students’ institutional and federal funding.”

Some students are demanding answers: “but what about our ability to eat?” But have the students considered: sustainability. With a probably unanimous decision by the sustainability initiatives, no food is better than wasted food and therefore it is more sustainable to remove all dining services. Alternatively, “students can eat at new Zamora’s hours from 8:59 a.m. to 9 a.m.” (Bone Appleteeth Cornell College).

Should students need to eat, they can if they forage for their own food. “It’s the ethical way to do it. It’s how I did it growing up after climbing up a mountain both ways to class,” the anonymous representative explained. “Fishing in Ink Pond is a great source of protein.” When asked about people with dietary restrictions, the representative shrugged and vanished into thin air.

Please refer to the Bone Appleteeth Cornell College’s website for more information about the daily meals. It will be empty.
